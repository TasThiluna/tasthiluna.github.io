KTaNE Modules

Solvable:

Module: Icon: Notes: Manual: Steam: Source:
Chinese Counting A decent debut in my opinion, although it has undergone several minor updates. Luckily, nobody noticed at the time that I was basically just following Royal's tutorial to a T, just with different aesthetics.
N&Ms A Pruz manual that I jumped at as my 2nd module because I wanted to experiment with arrays, specifically taking their differences, unions, and intersections. Ruleseed support added by Timwi.
Loopover A direct port of a puzzle of the same name by carykh, because I felt the game could use more easy solos. Looping animation by Timwi.
Object Shows For my first time constructing a more complicated solving process from scratch, I'm pretty proud of it. Contestant icons by EpicToast.
Scavenger Hunt The premise of navigating a maze to find clues that narrow a solution down to exactly one position came from Timwi's friend Ignatz. Before a big update, this module was significantly easier, with there only being one possible glyph for each possible clue.
Polygons This was originally a manual challenge, which was a very poor decision on my end. The challenge was aborted early. I'm still decently proud of the module as it stands by itself, however.
Codenames This module recieved a lot of negative attention upon its release, but I still stand by it. I mostly made this because I wanted to experiment with looping coroutines.
Boxing Another module that's recieved negative criticism, but after a large rework relatively recently, I consider it a solid module. I'm also proud of having made the first module to use interaction punches as part of the solving process.
Topsy Turvy This was made because of a comment about how I was almost releasing one module every day, so I said, "how about two per day?". The solving process of the finished version differs slightly from Pruz's original manual, but I personally believe this to have been the better execution.
Semamorse A vague idea I had since before I had ever touched Unity. Some people really like this module, but I'm personally not as proud of the solving process as I am the visuals.
Symbolic Tasha A Pruz manual that I implemented because I thought another member of the Tasha family was long overdue, and this was my favorite of the available ones. This might actually see a rework at some point in the future, because I take issue with the balance of the table conditions.
Widdershins A short and sweet manual from EpicToast. Not much to say about this one- it's just a decent solo.
Shell Game Implementing Shell Game came to me when I, for some reason, decided I wanted to implement a manual by an old-timey KTaNEr who hadn't been seen around in forever, so I chose the author of S.E.T., Zawu. This is widely considered to be a very solid module and I have to agree.
The Samsung Another idea from before I was even close to modding, like Semamorse. I turned to Deaf for help with some apps I couldn't think of ideas for. This was in development for a long long time, starting in December of 2019 and releasing in March of 2020. I had a blast making this, from getting to experiment with a lot of programming concepts that were new to me, to picking out easter eggs and extra details.
Snowflakes My finding of the font came first, the solving process was an afterthought. I don't regret using the snowflakes whatsoever, but I think they deserved a better solving process. That being said, this has led to one of my favorite videos ever uploaded.
Exoplanets The third (and last) of the ideas from the Semamorse-Samsung era. I made the prefab around the same time I started work on The Samsung, to experiment with passing different objects into the same coroutine. EpicToast provided a manual built off my ideas later on.
M&Ns I don't know why I felt like turning N&Ms into a family, but I did. The 4 different possible rotations and 4 different possible letter combinations came naturally. The solving process was provided by Procyon.
IPA The first implemented manual from ItzShaun, somehow. Decent solo, was good to learn how to use mod settings. Not much else to say.
Dumb Waiters Made for Deaf's Trivia Murder Party 2 pack. I doubted myself a lot while coming up with the solving process, but it seems to have had an alright reception.
Tenpins The first ever implemented manual from Vinco, who has now became a very frequent partner of mine.
M&Ms An idea, once again, provided by Procyon. Ruleseed support by Timwi. I like the solving process on this one, I think it's Grid Matching done in a way that's more enjoyable to me.
Devilish Eggs Made partially for the meme, partially to give myself a challenge, but I think the result turned out great. Dealing with rebellious eggs cartwheeling off the module, however, was not fun.
Ars Goetia Identification Ooooh boy. I naively thought that I could do an identification module better than anyone else, and was confident it would stand out just because it used symbols I found cool. It didn't. If anything it just made the trend worse.
N&Ns The final module of this family was loosely based on Not Who's on First. Some stage ideas were by Procyon, others by myself. Stage 2 used to be a completely different solving process involving the names of colors written in Morse Code. It sucked.
Sugar Skulls Another "font first, manual second" module, although I'm actually happier with this one than Snowflakes- I think the solving process has some simple charm that doesn't go overboard.
Faulty Chinese Counting This was intended to be released on the one year anniversary of Chinese Counting, but I didn't quite make it. Originally, this was going to only be the extra calculations without the quirks and called "cruel" instead of "faulty", but I couldn't resist adding the quirks after accidentally hiding one of the buttons and realizing there was potentially an idea in there.
Small Circle I'm personally surprised that nobody else had made a size variant of Big Circle until this. Not much to say about this one either, I think the solving process is decently cool.
Watching Paint Dry I don't know why I wanted to make this, I really don't. It lives up to its name. The manual is by IambicDiameter.
Cartinese Another miss, but an opportunity that me and Vinco couldn't pass up. This was originally going to be something similar to Brainfuck, using the plusses and asterisks of Carti's tweets.
Phones Made for the second wave of modules in Deaf's Trivia Murder Party 2 pack. I have no commentary to make on this module's solving process, to me it's the pinnacle of mediocre and I'm not really proud of it. The modeling and animating was a pain in the ass.
Mirror Made for the second wave of modules in Deaf's Trivia Murder Party 2 pack, and something I'm way happier with than Phones. This is essentially my take on making a more fun Font Select.
Boozlesnap A manual by Vinco, implemented for the 1st modding jam, with the prompt of 'board games'. It won. It took a few visual updates, but I am very pleased with how this module has turned out and consider it one of my best. I especially like the animations.
Melody Memory This was first released in mid-2020, but was taken down because of the amount of negative feedback it got. I reuploaded it about a year later, with the same ruleset but a much smaller selection of instruments, and I think it' a fine module now.
Antistress Based on the app of the same name. Has recieved several updates over time, and the response has been generally positive...
Piragua ...which I can't say the same for about Piragua. I had In the Heights fresh in my mind and wanted to replicate the elegance of modules like Netherite and Pointless machines, and oh did I... not do that at all.
Gendercipher The start of what I called the paying off of my debts to Vinco, and what I consider my best era. A lot of the Vinco modules from this era had manuals that existed for months that I wanted to make but had never gotten around to. This was going to be released for pride month, but I was late by just a dozen or so weeks.
Identification Crisis I am really, really happy with Identifcation Crisis. The visuals, sound design, solving process, and meta jokes- I think Vinco and I nailed it all, to brag just a bit.
Tasque Managing Deltarune chapter 2 was fresh in my mind. I think it's a decent module now, if not a big simple, but it's a great improvement over the terminally bugged and overly complex original version.
Quilting This was in the works for over a year, which makes some amount of sense looking at the solving Process. Proud of this one in concept, but it's not that fun to solve on voice bombs. The manual is by Moon, and old face whose attention I got when this released. He was happy.
Time Machine An old and simple module from EpicToast, which I modified slightly. It's alright.
Multitracking A Vinco manual that I personally like, but some people don't. I wanted to make a mod about animal tracks for a long time, he didn't come to me with the manual until much later.
Baffling Box The non-euclidean visuals were made as an experiment by KavinKul, and I later added a solving process. A decent solving process that I think is saved by the visuals.
Ro A solo I made as a challenge, as was my wont. Reception to this one was pretty positive, which I didn't expect upon releasing it.
ID Exchange A module based on The Genius by Grunkle Squeaky. Some people are always gonna take some points off just because it's a boss, but I think it's a good module after the visual rework.
Marco Polo Possibly the most unstable module of mine. It was originally a solvable with 7 possible directions that came out in early 2020, was then deleted because it was impossible, reuploaded as a needy with only 3 directions, deleted again once I believed eXish had gotten the solvable version working, and then finally ended up as a solvable module with 3 directions.
Clumsy Loopover I don't even know how to solve this one, but some people do! Based on the finger size setting from the original loopover puzzle.
Boomtar the Great Made for the 2nd modding jam, with the prompt of 'unusual interaction'. It won. Modeling this one was fun, the foam issue that still persists today was not. I'm sure there's at least one person out there who is physically attracted to Boomtar.
Bakery I wanted to use the sprites of Cookie Clicker for a while, but it took me a bit to settle on a solving process I was happy with. I think my approach to describing the cookies was done decently well, although the module can end up a bit too long.
Charms A Vinco manual that was sitting unimplemented for months, and also, one of my best modules. I'm happy with nearly everything here- the puzzles, the sound effects, the easter eggs, and the flavor in the spells.
Mister Softee Back to back genuinely great modules from Vinco. The original process of the pseudo-needy segment was slightly different. The use of the Spongebob Bar in the solving steps caught some people's attention.
Basegate I like this Vinco manual, but I don't blame people who don't. I especially like this whole era of modules from me (late 2021) because I put a lot of work into the visuals.
Face Off Speaking of visuals... This could be better, but I think it at least looks cool. I kinda wanted to challenge myself to make something orientation based, but that ended up not being a huge part of the module.
